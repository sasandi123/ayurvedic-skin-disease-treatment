Bridging AI and Ayurvedic Medicine

AyurDerma is a research-oriented system that combines deep learning image analysis with traditional Sri Lankan Ayurvedic (Hela Wedakama) knowledge to provide personalized herbal treatment guidance for common skin conditions. It is designed to make traditional herbal knowledge more accessible while integrating modern diagnostic technology.

settings How It Works

The system follows a four-stage pipeline from image upload to treatment recommendation.

1
image_search

Disease Classification

An EfficientNet deep learning model classifies the uploaded image as acne, eczema or ringworm.

2
monitor_heart

Severity Assessment

A second model assesses whether the condition is mild, moderate or severe using ordinal regression.

3
local_florist

Herb Ranking

A Random Forest model ranks suitable Ayurvedic herbs based on disease, severity, age and skin profile.

4
menu_book

Treatment Lookup

The top-ranked herb is matched to a validated Ayurvedic treatment database with preparation instructions.

psychology AI Models Used

AyurDerma uses three machine learning models trained on curated dermatology and Ayurvedic datasets.

healing Conditions Covered

AyurDerma currently supports diagnosis and treatment guidance for three common skin conditions:

Acne (Muhudu Roga) — A common condition causing pimples, blackheads and whiteheads, often related to excess sebum and bacterial growth. Ayurvedic herbs such as Neem, Turmeric and Sandalwood are used.

Eczema (Wigasuma) — A chronic inflammatory condition causing dry, itchy and inflamed patches of skin. Herbs such as Coconut Oil, Margosa and Aloe Vera are commonly recommended.

Ringworm (Panu / Dadru) — A fungal infection of the skin causing ring-shaped rashes. Neem Oil, Garlic and Turmeric are traditionally used for their antifungal properties.
