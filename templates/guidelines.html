Usage Guidelines

Read these guidelines before starting a diagnosis to get the most accurate results.

water_drop How to Identify Your Skin Type

Your skin type determines which herbal remedies are safest and most effective for you.

🟡 Oily Skin

  • Face looks shiny or greasy, especially on forehead, nose and chin (T-zone)
  • Pores appear large or visible
  • Prone to blackheads, whiteheads and acne breakouts
  • Makeup slides off quickly
  • Skin feels oily even a few hours after washing

🟢 Normal Skin

  • Skin feels balanced — not too oily or too dry
  • Pores are small and barely visible
  • Rarely has breakouts or flaky patches
  • Feels comfortable throughout the day
  • Minimal skin sensitivity to products or weather

🔵 Dry Skin

  • Skin feels tight, rough or flaky
  • Visible dry patches, especially around nose and cheeks
  • Pores appear very small or invisible
  • Feels uncomfortable or itchy after washing
  • Fine lines may appear more visible

science Simple Blotting Paper Test (Most Reliable)

  1. Wash your face gently with a mild cleanser and pat dry.
  2. Wait 1 hour without applying any products.
  3. Press a blotting paper or tissue on different areas of your face: forehead, nose, cheeks and chin.
  4. Hold the paper up to the light and observe the oil marks.
Lots of oil everywhere → Oily Little to no oil → Normal No oil, skin feels tight → Dry Oil on T-zone only → Combination (select Normal)

sentiment_very_dissatisfied How to Identify Skin Sensitivity

Sensitive skin requires milder herbal formulations to prevent adverse reactions.

✅ Normal (Non-Sensitive) Skin

  • Skin tolerates most skincare products without reaction
  • No burning or stinging sensation after applying products
  • No redness or rash from weather changes
  • Rarely develops allergic reactions to herbal or natural products
  • Recovers quickly after sun exposure or environmental stress

⚠️ Sensitive Skin

  • Skin reacts to new products with redness, itching or burning
  • Easily irritated by fragrances, dyes or harsh ingredients
  • Flushes or becomes blotchy in hot/cold weather
  • Stings or tingles when applying most skincare products
  • History of eczema, rosacea or allergic skin conditions

colorize Patch Test for Sensitivity (Recommended Before Any Remedy)

  1. Apply a small amount of the herbal preparation on the inner wrist or behind the ear.
  2. Leave it on for 24 hours without washing.
  3. Check for any redness, itching, swelling or burning sensation.
  4. If any reaction occurs, do not use that preparation and consult a doctor.
  5. If no reaction occurs, the preparation is safe to use on the affected area.

camera_alt How to Take a Good Photo for Diagnosis

Photo quality directly affects the accuracy of the AI diagnosis.

wb_sunny

Good Lighting

Use natural daylight or bright indoor light. Avoid dark rooms or harsh flash which creates glare.

zoom_in

Close but Clear

Get close enough to show the affected area clearly, but make sure the image is in focus and not blurry.

crop_free

Show the Full Affected Area

Ensure the entire rash, patch or affected skin is visible and centred in the frame.

block

No Makeup or Cream

Remove any makeup, creams or ointments from the affected area before taking the photo.

stay_current_portrait

Hold the Camera Steady

Rest your hand or phone against a surface to avoid motion blur. Take multiple shots and choose the sharpest.

color_lens

True Colours

Avoid filters or editing. The AI needs the natural colour of the skin lesion for accurate classification.

warning
